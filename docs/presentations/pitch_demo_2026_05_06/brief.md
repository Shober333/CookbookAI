# CookbookAI Pitch Demo Brief

---

## Audience

A product or demo audience evaluating whether CookbookAI is clear,
useful, and worth validating with real recipe sources.

## Goal

Show CookbookAI as a coherent cooking workflow: import from the internet,
save to a private library, adapt to the user's kitchen, and handle messy
source paths with discipline.

## Narrative Spine

1. Home cooks discover recipes everywhere, but cook from scattered
   bookmarks, videos, screenshots, and notes.
2. CookbookAI collapses discovery, extraction, saving, and adaptation into
   one kitchen loop.
3. The demo should show real product states, not generic AI claims.
4. Source handling matters: YouTube and blocked recipe pages are treated as
   explicit source paths, not vague magic.
5. Adaptation is the wedge: the app rewrites cooking method steps around
   the equipment a user actually has.
6. The AI boundary is constrained by source metadata, structured output,
   provider configuration, and user-owned recipes.
7. Sprint 7 extends the loop with macro estimates, optional direct video
   fallback, and Groq text-provider work.
8. The pitch ask is to validate the kitchen loop with real recipe URLs,
   YouTube videos, and pasted recipes.

## Design Direction

- Warm Domestic with editorial discipline, echoing `docs/ui/REGISTER.md`.
- Cream paper, ink brown, terracotta as the AI/adaptation signal.
- Product screenshots as proof objects.
- Sparse claim titles, light diagramming, no generic AI imagery.
- No fabricated metrics, logos, customer claims, or revenue assertions.
